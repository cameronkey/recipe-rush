Classic Margherita Pizza

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 15 mins Servings: 4 Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, yeast, and salt. Gradually add warm water and olive oil, mixing until a dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Place in a greased bowl, cover with a towel, and let rise for 1 hour in a warm place.
  4. Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C) with a pizza stone if available.
  5. Roll out the dough to your desired thickness and transfer to a pizza peel or baking sheet.
  6. Spread tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a border for the crust.
  7. Add torn mozzarella pieces and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Bake for 12-15 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
  9. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.
Chef's Tip: For the best results, let your dough rise overnight in the refrigerator. This develops more flavor and creates a better texture.

Chocolate Lava Cake

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 12 mins Servings: 2 Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 oz dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Butter for ramekins
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Butter two 6-ounce ramekins and dust with cocoa powder.
  2. In a double boiler, melt chocolate and butter together until smooth. Remove from heat.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, egg yolks, and sugar until pale and thick.
  4. Fold the chocolate mixture into the egg mixture until well combined.
  5. Gently fold in flour and salt until just combined.
  6. Divide batter between prepared ramekins.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes until the edges are set but the center is still soft.
  8. Let stand for 1 minute, then run a knife around the edges and invert onto plates.
  9. Serve immediately with fresh berries or vanilla ice cream.
Chef's Tip: Don't overbake! The center should still be slightly jiggly when you remove it from the oven. The residual heat will continue cooking it to the perfect consistency.

Thai Green Curry

Prep Time: 20 mins Cook Time: 25 mins Servings: 6 Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp green curry paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, bamboo shoots, green beans)
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 2 red chilies, sliced
  • Steamed jasmine rice for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat a large wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of the coconut milk and fry the curry paste for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the remaining coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add chicken pieces and cook for 5-6 minutes until almost cooked through.
  5. Add vegetables and continue cooking for 3-4 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting to taste.
  7. Stir in Thai basil and sliced chilies just before serving.
  8. Serve hot over steamed jasmine rice.
Chef's Tip: For authentic flavor, use fresh green curry paste. You can find it in Asian grocery stores, or make your own by grinding fresh green chilies, lemongrass, and herbs.
